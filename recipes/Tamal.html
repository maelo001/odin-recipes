Tamal

When it comes to Latin American cuisine, nothing beats traditional tamales colombianos!

Featuring a fluffy and soft tamal dough mixed with rice and peas flavored with cumin and color, also known as achiote, this traditional Colombian tamal is stuffed with marinated pork and chicken for an authentic Colombian dish definitely worth trying.

Ingridients

  • 1/2 onions, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 4 green onions, or chives, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 2 pimentones, cut in half, for the marinade
  • 1/2 cups parsley, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 2 tomatoes, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 3 cloves garlic, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 1/2 tablespoons ground cumin, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 1/2 teaspoons salt, cut into quarters, for the marinade
  • 1/2 teaspoons achiote, ground, for the marinade
  • 1/2 cups water
  • 4 chicken drumsticks
  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 400 grams pork
  • 400 grams pork ribs
  • 400 grams pancetta
  • 2 1/2 cups rice, rinsed and drained
  • 5 1/2 cups broth, for cooking the meat and for the rice
  • 1/2 teaspoons cumin, for the rice
  • salt, for the rice
  • pepper, for the rice
  • achiote, ground, for the rice
  • 3 1/2 cups broth, for cooking the meat, for the tamal dough
  • vegetable oil, with achiote, for brushing the banana leaves
  • banana leaf, charred
  • 1/2 cups peas, precooked, for the tamal dough
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, for the hogao or sofrito
  • 1 cup green onion, finely chopped, for the hogao or sofrito
  • 2 cups tomato, finely chopped, for the hogao or sofrito
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped, for the hogao or sofrito
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin, for the hogao or sofrito
  • 1/4 teaspoons salt, for the hogao or sofrito
  • 2 eggs, cut into round slices, for assembling the tamales
  • 1 carrot, cut into thin round slices, for assembling the tamales
  • 4 red potatoes, cut into thin round slices, for assembling the tamales
  • parsley, leaves, for assembling the tamales
  • water, for cooking the tamales

Steps

  1. For the marinade: In a blender, combine onion, green onion, piment√≥n, parsley, tomatoes, garlic, salt, ground achiote, and cumin and blend for 5 minutes until smooth. Set aside.
  2. In a bowl, combine chicken, pork, pork ribs, and pancetta. Pour in blended mixture, cover with plastic wrap, and let marinate overnight.
  3. Remove meat from the marinade and remove excess marinade. In a pot with plenty of boiling water, cook meat for 40 minutes or until tender. Remove meat and set strained broth aside.
  4. Pour broth into a pot and add rice, salt, pepper, cumin, and ground achiote. Cook over medium heat for 15 minutes or until water evaporates. Set aside.
  5. In a bowl, mix broth for the tamal dough and corn masa flour until a semi-thick mixture is obtained. Add ground achiote and mix thoroughly.
  6. Add tamal dough and peas to the previously cooked rice. If the tamal dough is a bit dry, add a bit of melted lard. Set aside.
  7. In a skillet, heat oil and cook tomato, onion, and garlic for 15 minutes. Season with salt and set aside.
  8. Brush banana leaves with oil. Spread a bed of tamal dough on each banana leaf forming a 0.3-inch-thick square at the center.
  9. Stuff tamal dough with hogao, chicken, pork, pork rib, pancetta, potatoes, and carrots. Top with a bit of hogao, parsley leaves, and egg round slices. Place more tamal dough on top and wrap the banana leaf tightly to keep the tamal colombiano from spilling and so water does not come in. Using kitchen twine, gently close each tamal colombiano without breaking the banana leaves.
  10. Cook traditional tamal colombiano in a steamer over medium heat for 90 minutes
  11. Serve Colombian tamales in the banana leaf on a plate.
